Texas Toast Recipe

Description

This homemade Texas Toast garlic bread is made on the stovetop with just 3 ingredients. It’s thick and soft and buttery, and perfectly golden brown.

Soft and buttery, golden brown slices of Texas Toast garlic bread are so hard to resist. It’s total comfort food and goes with everything from salad to soup to pasta, to anything hot off the backyard grill!

Ingredients

  • Butter – Use salted butter for the extra flavor.
  • Garlic Powder – This adds delicious savory flavor. Make sure it’s not clumpy, which tends to happen when garlic powder sits for a while.
  • Bread – You can use Texas Toast or pull-apart bread.

Steps to Make It

  1. Make the butter melt.

    2. Melt 4 tablespoons of butter in a small bowl then stir in the garlic powder and brush both sides of the bread with the garlic butter.

  2. Heat the skillet.

    3. Heat a griddle or skillet on the stovetop (or use an electric griddle) over medium heat.

    Then add a tablespoon of butter (butter is a common theme with Texas Toast!) and swirl it around to evenly coat the skillet surface.

  3. Cook each slice of bread.

    4. Place the buttered bread on the heated griddle. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes per side, or until each side is your desired shade of golden brown.
