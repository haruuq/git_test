Salt and Pepper Spareribs Recipe

Description

Chinese Salt and Pepper Spare Ribs are a popular Cantonese dish featuring crispy, deep-fried pork ribs seasoned with a homemade pepper salt blend and fresh aromatics. a

The preparation typically involves marinating the ribs in rice wine, soy sauce, and spices, coating them in potato starch or cornstarch, and then deep-frying them twice to ensure a crunchy exterior and tender interior.

Ingredients

  • 10 ounces/280 grams spareribs or pork shoulder
  • 2 tablespoons sweet potato starch or cornflour
  • 1 scallion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablesppon plain four
  • 1 tablespoon black vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 3 to 4 cups of oil, or as needed to deep-fry the spare-ribs
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine or Shaoxing rice wine

Steps to Make It

Make the Chinese Pepper Salt

  1. Gather the ingredients.

  2. Put Sichuan peppercorns and salt into a small, dry saucepan over medium to low heat and keep stirring while cooking.

    This procedure is to toast the Sichuan peppercorns together with the salt so that the salt absorbs the aroma and flavor of the peppercorns. Toast for 2 to 3 minutes.

  3. Use a sieve to separate the Sichuan peppercorns from the salt.

    (You can use the leftover Sichuan peppercorns to make Sichuan pepercorn oil)

  4. Mix the salt with ground black pepper.

Make the Spare Ribs

  1. Gather the Ingredients

  2. Combine the marinade ingredients in a large bowl and stir well.

  3. Cut the spareribs into individual ribs and place them in the bowl with the marinade for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for more flavor.

    Make sure to stir it at least a couple of times during the marinating process to help the spareribs absorb all the flavor.

  4. Heat the oil in a wok to 350F/180 C.

    Working in batches, deep fry the spareribs for a minute and turn off the heat and leave it in the oil to cook for 5 minutes.

  5. Drain and leave the ribs aside.

  6. Put scallion, garlic, and chili in a big, heatproof mixing bowl.

  7. Heat up the oil again to 350 F/180 C and deep-fry the spareribs in batches for another 3 to 4 minutes or until they are crispy.

  8. Drain and put the ribs into the mixing bowl with the scallion, garlic, and chili.

  9. Add some Chinese Pepper Salt, to taste, and quickly toss everything a few times.
